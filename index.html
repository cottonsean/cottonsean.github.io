About Me

JSU Alum MBA Alpha Phi Alpha Army Veteran Dog Dad x4 Married Man of Faith

I'm Sean Cotton. Software engineer at SMC³, Army veteran, JSU alum, Alpha man. I grew up in Chicago but Mississippi raised me — and those roots follow me everywhere I go.

I didn't come up through tech. Ten years as a Military Police Officer, then twenty years in sales — Cox Automotive, Nestlé Waters, CarMax. In 2017 I started teaching HTML and CSS to middle schoolers at a prep school in Atlanta. That's when I knew. I've been building things ever since.

Today I build enterprise software at SMC³. When I'm not at the keyboard, you'll find me with Dr. Wendy Cotton, spoiling Sasha, Alpha, Symba, and Nalah, or parked in front of the Big Green Egg. I've also got strong opinions about landscape lighting and what temperature a brisket should be before it ever comes off the grill. I'm a man of faith, and I know every word to Paid in Full — just ask Dr. Wendy.

Early Years

Me & my brother Daryl

I was born in Chicago, Illinois — but Mississippi made me. My family put roots down in Meridian, Mississippi, and that's where I came of age under the watch of my great-aunt Alice Hubbard — Aunt Luch. She ran a neighborhood grocery store, took me fishing, taught me to cook, and wrapped our whole world in love. Meridian also shaped my discipline through Meridian High School's JROTC program, planting seeds that would eventually lead me to commission as an Army officer.

Summers meant the bus between Chicago and Mississippi — and if you grew up doing it, you know. Cold fried chicken on white bread was the road food. Chicago gave me the city. Mississippi gave me the roots. Both made me who I am.

From Meridian, I headed to Jackson and enrolled at Jackson State University — one of the great HBCUs, and the place that truly changed my life. At JSU I found my brotherhood, crossing into Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc. alongside 17 others. I earned my degree at JSU and went on to earn my MBA, building the academic foundation I still draw on today.

The Journey

1987 – 1997

The Army Years

Commissioned as a Military Police Officer out of Jackson State University, I served 10 years and rose to Lieutenant. I led 33 soldiers, managed a $200K operating budget, and was responsible for $5M in property across a 72-acre facility. I was 18 years old when I started. The Army will do that to you.

1997 – 2017

Twenty Years in Sales

After the Army, I spent two decades in sales — Cox Automotive, Nestlé Waters, CarMax. Different industries, same skill: figuring out what someone actually needs before you open your mouth. Twenty years of that stays with you. I still write code the same way I used to run a sales call. You listen first.

2017 – 2019

The Pivot

In 2017 I walked into Ivy Preparatory Academy and started teaching HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to middle schoolers. I had no idea what I was doing at first. But I loved it. When I saw those kids build something that worked, I wanted that feeling for myself. I went and got it.

2019 – Present

We Did It, Mom

When I was young, my mother — Lillian Opal Cotton — used to tell me I should become an engineer. She spent over 25 years working at Sargent & Lundy, a major engineering firm in Chicago. She saw something in me I wasn't ready to see in myself. I majored in Political Science. I went into the Army. I spent two decades in sales. I held onto what she said, just between her and me — and I never let it go.

In 2019 I joined SMC³ as a Technical Support Analyst and kept climbing — Integration Support Engineer, agile teams, thousands of hours of Java, Apache Camel, Spring Boot, and Oracle. On August 24, 2022, my manager called an unexpected Zoom meeting and told me I was being promoted to Software Engineer.

Mama had been gone a few years by then. I sat there on that Zoom call thinking about her. Couldn't say much.

To God be the glory. We did it, Mom.
